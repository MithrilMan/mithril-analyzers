## Facts

- `BGA001` counts physical file lines and ignores generated files by suffix or `<auto-generated` marker.
- `BGA002` covers methods, constructors, and local functions with block bodies and intentionally skips lambdas.
- `BGA003` checks `get` and `set` accessors with block bodies; `init` remains a future compatibility enhancement.
- `BGA004` reports `Task`/`ValueTask` methods that expose no cancellation path unless exclusions apply.
- `BGA005` only flags simple `ContainsKey` followed by indexer patterns and skips compound conditions or `if` statements with `else`.

## Implications

- The shipped heuristics are conservative by design and may miss edge cases rather than create noisy warnings.
- When V2 rules start, they should keep the same low-noise bias and documented exclusion policy.

## Follow-ups

- Add `init` accessor coverage when the analyzer baseline or target Roslyn surface can support it cleanly.
- Design `BGA100+` with the same conservative-first approach.
